Overview

Soundproof-ready flex room at the south end of the first floor. Right now: a reading library with full-wall bookshelves and a deep window seat facing south. Later: home recording studio, podcast room, or home cinema — the double-wall cavity and solid-core acoustic door make the conversion simple. No permanent fixtures that prevent the change.

Interior Design Note The full-wall bookshelves (north and east walls) are teak — adjustable peg shelving, 35cm deep. A deep window seat (60cm deep) runs the full south wall width — under-seat storage with lift-up lid. The midnight blue south wall is a reference to the deep night sky — a reading room should feel like it has no ceiling. A single Anglepoise-style brass wall light over the window seat. The acoustic conversion involves: installing rockwool in the double-wall cavity (pre-designed), adding acoustic foam panels to ceiling, done. No structural changes needed.
