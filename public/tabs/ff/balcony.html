Overview

South-facing open balcony — the traditional Tamil "Thotti." Looks directly onto the compound entry and the road beyond. Traditional wrought iron grille parapet with a kolam-pattern infill. Kota stone floor. A long metal drying rod runs the full width. Two low chairs fit comfortably for evening sitting. The balcony opens from the master bedroom via French doors.

Interior Design Note The wrought iron grille is hand-forged — a kolam pattern specific to this house, matching the stair railing and Nadumuttam railing. The parapet top has a 15cm flat Kota stone cap that doubles as a planter ledge — terracotta pots with jasmine and money plant. The drying rod is a single 2.8m stainless steel rod, wall-to-wall, at 1.9m height. Two low plantation chairs in teak. The French door from master has full-height glass panels — when open, the bedroom and balcony feel continuous.
