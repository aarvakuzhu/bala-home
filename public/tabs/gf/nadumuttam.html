Overview

The Nadumuttam is an open-to-sky internal courtyard — a traditional Tamil vernacular element. Rain falls directly in. Morning sunlight sweeps across the Cuddapah stone floor. A Tulsi plant grows in a circular planter at centre. The sliding glass wall connects it to the Dining Hall — when open, the two spaces merge. Rainwater drains to an underground sump for reuse.

Interior Design Note The Cuddapah stone floor has a shallow central depression — water flows naturally to the drain channel without visible slope. The circular Tulsi planter is 60cm diameter, raised 30cm — hand-chiselled black granite. The ornamental iron railing at FF level is a kolam-pattern infill — matching the dining stair railing. When the sliding glass is fully open, the Nadumuttam becomes an extension of the dining room, and the sky is the ceiling.
