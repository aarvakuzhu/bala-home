Overview

The heart of the home. A 21 ft double-height ceiling opens to the first floor — no slab, full sky above through a rooftop skylight. The stair wraps two faces of this room (north and west), making the act of ascending and descending a visible event from the dining table. The Nadumuttam courtyard opens via sliding glass to the east — rain, sunlight, and Tulsi scent reach the dining table.

Interior Design Note Three large Athangudi pendants hang at 12 ft — mid-void — brass frame with amber glass. The stair railing is hand-forged wrought iron with traditional kolam-pattern infill. The dining table is custom teak, 2.4m long, seats 8–10. The sliding glass to the Nadumuttam is 1.8m wide — fully stackable. When open, the rain sounds, plant scent, and natural light make this the most alive room in the house.
